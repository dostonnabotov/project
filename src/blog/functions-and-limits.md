## Functions

### Odd Function

> $f(-x) = -f(x)$

**Example #1:**

$$ f(x) = x^{3} + \sin^{2}x + \frac{\cos x}{x} $$

**Solution:**

$$ f(-x) = (-x)^{3} + \sin^{2}(-x) + \frac{\cos (-x)}{(-x)} $$

$$ f(-x) = -x^{3} - \sin^{2}x - \frac{\cos x}{x} $$

$$ f(-x) = -(x^{3} + \sin^{2}x + \frac{\cos x}{x}) $$

$$ f(-x) = -f(x) $$

### Even Function

> $f(-x) = f(x)$

**Example #2:**

$$ f(x) = x^4 - 3x^2 + 7 $$

**Solution:**

$$ f(-x) = (-x)^4 - 3(-x)^2 + 7 $$

$$ f(-x) = x^4 - 3x^2 + 7 $$

$$ f(-x) = f(x) $$

### Neither Odd nor Even Function

> $f(-x) \ne -f(x)$ and $f(-x) \ne f(x)$

**Example #3:**

$$ f(x) = \sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{(1-x)^2} $$

> **Pay attention:** $ 1-x^2 $ and $ (1-x)^2 $ are not the same things.

**Solution:**

$$ f(-x) = \sqrt{1-(-x)^2} + \sqrt{(1-(-x))^2} $$

$$ f(-x) = \sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{(1+x)^2} $$

$$ f(-x) \ne -f(x) \ and \ f(-x) \ne f(x) $$
